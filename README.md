# Loblaw Companies Limited — Corporate Risk Assessment Portfolio

**Personal Portfolio Project**

A simulated corporate risk assessment portfolio modeled after real-world internal audit and enterprise risk management (ERM) practices. All risk data is sourced directly from Loblaw Companies Limited's publicly available 2025 Annual Report — no data was fabricated.

---

## Project Overview

This project applies risk identification, assessment, and reporting skills to a real Canadian publicly traded company. Using Loblaw's 2025 Annual Report as the primary data source, the project simulates the work an internal audit or risk management team would perform when evaluating enterprise-level risks.

---

| Tool | Purpose |

---

## Deliverables

### 1. Risk Register (Excel)
A structured register identifying key enterprise risks extracted from Loblaw's ERM disclosures. Each risk is rated by likelihood and impact, producing a risk priority score and heat map classification.

### 2. Risk Assessment Report (PDF)
A formal written report analyzing Loblaw's top enterprise risks, their potential financial and operational impact, and recommended risk responses. Structured to reflect professional internal audit reporting standards.

### 3. Power BI Dashboard (.pbix + Screenshots)
An interactive dashboard visualizing the risk register data. Includes risk heat maps, priority rankings by category, and a risk distribution overview by business segment. The `.pbix` file is included so the dashboard can be opened and explored in Power BI Desktop (free download from Microsoft).

---

## Key Risks Analyzed

The following risks were identified directly from Section 11 of Loblaw's 2025 Annual Report (Enterprise Risks and Risk Management):

1. **Cybersecurity, Privacy and Data Breaches** — dependence on uninterrupted IT systems and exposure of confidential customer and financial data
2. **Economic Conditions** — inflation, tariffs, interest rate changes, and shifts in consumer spending behaviour
3. **Competitive Environment and Strategy** — pressure from supermarkets, mass merchandisers, warehouse clubs, and e-commerce retailers
4. **IT Systems Implementations and Data Management** — risks associated with large-scale technology transformation
5. **Distribution and Supply Chain** — reliance on third-party providers and logistics infrastructure
6. **Labour Relations** — outcomes of collective bargaining and colleague attraction and retention
7. **Food, Drug, Product and Services Safety** — product liability, recall risk, and medication dispensing errors
8. **Electronic Commerce and Disruptive Technologies** — execution risk around Loblaw's digital retail and omnichannel strategy
9. **Regulatory Compliance** — exposure to changing laws across food, drug, financial services, and environmental sectors
10. **Environmental and Social** — climate change adaptation and social equity commitments
